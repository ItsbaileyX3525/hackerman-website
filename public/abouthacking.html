What is hacking?

Hacking is the act of exploiting a computer system or network to gain unauthorized access to data or systems. It can be used for both ethical and unethical purposes.

Unethical hacking such as doing hacking without permissions is illegal as you may have guessed and falls under the GDPR and computer misuse act which can cost you a rather pretty penny if caught.

Ethical hacking is the act of exploiting a computer system or network to gain unauthorized access to data or systems with permission from the owner. This is often done to identify and fix security vulnerabilities.

A little history of hacking.

Hacking has been around since the early days of computing, with the term "hacker" originally referring to someone who enjoyed exploring and tinkering with computer systems. However, as computers became more widespread, the term began to take on a more negative connotation, particularly with the rise of cybercrime in the 1990s.

In the early days of the internet, hacking was often seen as a way to push the boundaries of technology and explore new possibilities. However, as the internet became more commercialized, hacking began to be associated with criminal activity, such as identity theft and data breaches.

Today, hacking is a complex and multifaceted field, with both ethical and unethical hackers working to exploit vulnerabilities in computer systems and networks. While some hackers use their skills for malicious purposes, others work to improve security and protect against cyber threats.
